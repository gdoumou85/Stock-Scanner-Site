## The Basics

SOFI is a digital banking company founded in 2011. They offer student loan refinancing, personal loans, banking accounts, investing, and credit cards. They went public via SPAC in 2021 at $10 per share and ran up to nearly $25 earlier this year.

Now the stock is at $15.22, down 53% from its high of $32.73 in November.

---

## Why We Are Watching This Now

Three things caught our attention:

**1. The Price Action**
The stock has fallen hard and fast. When something drops 50% in four months, it eventually gets interesting. Not necessarily as a buy, but as something to watch for a potential bounce.

**2. The Short Situation**
In early March, short seller Muddy Waters published a report claiming SOFI has accounting problems and regulatory risks. The stock dropped 15% that day and has struggled since.

However, SOFI management pushed back hard, calling the claims misleading. They hired outside counsel to review the allegations. No SEC investigation has been announced.

**Source:** [CNBC - SOFI Responds to Short Seller Report](https://cnbc.com)

**3. The Valuation Reset**
At current prices, SOFI trades at about 2.5 times sales. That is down from over 5 times at the peak. For a company still growing revenue at 25% per year, that is starting to look reasonable.

---

## The Financial Picture

**Revenue:** $3.4 billion over the last 12 months, up 26% from the year before
**Members:** 7.5 million total, up 35% year-over-year
**Profit:** The company turned profitable on a GAAP basis in Q4 2024
**Cash:** $2.1 billion on hand, plenty to fund operations
**Debt:** $3.8 billion, but manageable for a growing financial company

**Source:** SOFI Q4 2024 earnings report

The business is actually doing fine. Member growth is strong, they are adding new products, and they just hit profitability. The stock price does not reflect that.

---

## What Could Go Right

- **Short squeeze:** With 12% of shares sold short, any good news could force short sellers to buy back shares quickly
- **Earnings beat:** Q1 results come out April 27. A strong quarter could change sentiment
- **Banking charter execution:** SOFI bought a small bank to get a national charter. If that works out, it lowers their funding costs
- **Sector rotation:** If investors rotate back into fintech, SOFI would benefit

---

## What Could Go Wrong

- **Student loan pressures:** The Biden administration keeps changing student loan rules, which hurts SOFI's core refinancing business
- **Competition:** Traditional banks are getting better at digital, and other fintechs are fierce competitors
- **Interest rates:** If rates stay high longer, borrowing slows and SOFI makes less money
- **More short attacks:** Muddy Waters or others could pile on with more negative reports
